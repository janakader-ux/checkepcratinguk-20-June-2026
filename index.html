EPC Ratings

Understanding the EPC Rating Scale

Every property is rated from A (most efficient) to G (least efficient). The minimum for rental properties is E.

🏠 For Homeowners

A higher EPC rating means lower energy bills and a higher property value. Simple improvements like loft insulation and LED lighting can boost your rating significantly.

🔑 For Landlords

Your rental property must have a minimum E rating under MEES regulations. Properties rated F or G cannot be legally let without a valid exemption.

📋 For Sellers

An EPC is legally required before marketing your property. A better rating makes your home more attractive to buyers and can speed up the sale.

🏢 For Property Managers

Track EPCs across your portfolio to stay compliant. We offer bulk assessment rates and can help you plan improvements to meet future C-rating targets.
