Commercial (non-domestic) EPC assessments in Bedford

A commercial EPC — properly called a non-domestic EPC — is required whenever a commercial building is constructed, sold or let. It rates the building's energy efficiency from A (best) to G (worst) based on a detailed SBEM (Simplified Building Energy Model) assessment of the fabric, services and controls. The certificate is valid for 10 years and must be lodged on the official EPC Register.

We produce commercial EPCs for clients across Bedford, including the following building types:

  • Offices — small single-tenant units up to medium-sized office floors.
  • Retail — high-street shops, small shopping parades, independent retail.
  • HMOs assessed as commercial — large HMOs (typically 6+ bedrooms) where the property is treated as non-domestic.
  • Warehouses and industrial units — including light industrial and trade counters.
  • Restaurants, cafés and takeaways.
  • Serviced offices and co-working spaces.

How a commercial EPC differs from a domestic EPC

This is the single most common source of confusion. A domestic EPC is produced using RdSAP, a reduced method for existing dwellings. A commercial EPC is produced using SBEM, which models the building's heating, cooling, hot water, lighting, ventilation, and associated controls separately. A different qualification is required to produce each — we are qualified for domestic assessments and will connect you with our commercial partner for larger, level-5 non-domestic jobs.

What MEES means for commercial landlords in Bedford

The Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards for non-domestic property tightened significantly in April 2023. The headline points for any landlord letting property in Bedford:

  • It is unlawful to continue to let a property with an EPC rating of F or G, whether the tenancy is new or ongoing — unless a valid exemption is registered on the PRS Exemptions Register.
  • The government has consulted on raising the minimum rating to C by 2027 and B by 2030. Landlords with older EPCs rated D and E should start planning upgrades now.
  • Breaches can result in publication of a penalty notice and fines of up to 20% of the property's rateable value (capped at £150,000 per breach).

Thinking of buying a commercial property in Bedford? Get the EPC produced as part of your due diligence — it often uncovers cost-effective upgrades (lighting, controls, insulation) that pay back inside 3 years and can add value at sale.

Exemptions from commercial EPCs

Not every non-domestic building needs an EPC. The main exemptions:

  • Places of worship.
  • Temporary buildings used for less than 2 years.
  • Listed or protected buildings where compliance would unacceptably alter character.
  • Industrial sites with low energy demand (typical large warehouses with no heating/cooling of space).
  • Stand-alone buildings with a total useful floor area under 50 m² that are not dwellings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a commercial EPC cost in Bedford?

Commercial EPCs in Bedford are priced per building based on floor area, complexity and the type of building services installed. Small retail units typically start from £200; larger offices and warehouses are quoted individually. Call 07345 295 687 for a fixed price.

What is the difference between a domestic and commercial EPC?

A domestic EPC is produced using the RdSAP methodology for dwellings. A commercial (non-domestic) EPC uses SBEM (Simplified Building Energy Model) and includes the building's heating, cooling, hot water, lighting and, where relevant, ventilation systems. They are different qualifications and different software.

What is MEES for commercial landlords?

The Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards for non-domestic property came into force from April 2023: you cannot continue to let a commercial property with an EPC rating of F or G, whether or not it is a new tenancy, unless a valid exemption is registered on the PRS Exemptions Register.

Does my building need a commercial EPC?

A commercial EPC is required when a non-domestic building is constructed, sold or let. There are exemptions for places of worship, industrial sites with low energy demand, and some listed buildings — we'll confirm when we quote.
